Budget Ease

An app for millennials to easily and conveniently track their spending and create budget goals. This case study goes through the initial problem research to user testing one feature of the conceptual app.


The Discovery Process

It all started a year after graduating college, I decided to move out of my parent’s house and live on my own. As a young working professional, I thought I saved enough of my income to be self-sufficient and financially independent. Soon after the move, I learned that it was very difficult to budget my spending and track my personal finances. I spent a lot of time thinking about how I should better my financial health for the future, but I didn’t know how.

Initial Problem Statement

Millennials need a way to keep track of their personal finances because they want to save for big investments in the future, like purchasing a house.

Competitor Analysis

Key Findings: Learned that competitors do not necessarily have to be companies or products that are related to the same topic, such as finances. Instead, I discovered competitors can be companies or services that take the time of user’s that you also want to target. This was an eye opener to me and taught me to think more broadly about who/what competitors can be. Thinking this way made me realize mindfulness apps, like Headspace, is a competitor to financial apps since they provide a service that people want to use and feel good about using, which is usually the opposite sentiment of using a financial app.

User Research

After the competitive analysis, I wanted to learn more about user goals. To further my research, I conducted user interviews and gave out surveys. My goal was to find out what do people want and what do they need?

User Testing

I conducted a user test with my first prototype of the game application. After observing the users' behaviors and asking them questions about their user experiences, I learned how I can improve the app and make it stronger.

My Role

Researcher, Designer, and Developer.

The Challenge

Develop an engaging interface that teaches UC Davis community members how to properly dispose of their waste on campus.

The Solution

An interactive and educational game app that teaches users the best way to sort and dispose of their waste on campus.

Future updates of this app will contain a search system and index that will tell users which bin to use for their searched item.
